## global-daily-template.md
# Global Daily Template

Use this template for the standing daily WeChat column `《环球日报》` published at `08:00` China time.

## Best Use Cases

- `《环球日报》` daily issue
- Stable recurring morning edition
- Habit-forming daily newsroom rhythm

## Structure

1. Fixed title in the format `《环球日报》｜X月X日`
2. One light metadata line with information cutoff, and optional read-time estimate
3. One fixed first-screen module: `先看三句`
4. One short lead that explains the day's main signal
5. `Today's 3 most important stories`
6. `2-3` grouped sections for the rest of the article
7. One fixed `经济投资风向标` section
8. `What to watch today`
9. Optional image suggestions
10. Source summary

## Article Skeleton

### Title

- Use the exact title pattern `《环球日报》｜X月X日` unless the user overrides it

### Metadata line

- Put one light metadata line under the title
- Default content:
  - information cutoff
  - optional read-time estimate when helpful
- Keep the tone neutral and useful, not promotional

### 先看三句

- Put this directly under the title
- Use exactly `3` short bullets or lines
- Each line should contain one concrete fact, stake, or shift, not a vague mood summary
- Make the block readable in about `10` seconds on a phone
- Do not simply repeat the lead in shorter wording
- When the day supports it, prefer this three-part mix:
  - one shared-agenda or conflict fact
  - one market, cost, or transmission fact
  - one domestic rule, policy, or public-value fact

### Lead

- One short paragraph
- Explain the common thread of the day
- Front-load one hard fact, named actor, or concrete stake before abstract synthesis when possible
- Frame why a China-based morning reader should care now
- The lead should be easy to enter for a broad reader but still point toward the more editor-like logic of the body

### Today's 3 most important stories

For each item include:

- `Headline`
- `What happened`: one short factual block that states the new development cleanly
- `Concrete facts`: one short block with the key actor, figure, timing, or decision
- `Key friction / next step`: one short block that says what is unresolved, what changed overnight, or what the reader should watch next
- `Why it matters / who it hits`: one short block that explains where the impact transmits next and who feels it first
- `Source / date`

Treat `Top 3` as concrete information blocks, not as compressed clips. They should be visibly more detailed than the grouped-section items and should contain named actors plus at least one hard fact.
One event chain should usually occupy only one `Top 3` slot. If two candidate items are really the same story with different angles, merge them into one stronger explainer.
Let item `1` feel like the clear lead and let items `2-3` widen the reader's picture of the day rather than echo it.
If the day supports it, enrich major `Top 3` items with one more layer such as `why now`, a transmission chain, or a market mechanism before adding extra low-value items elsewhere.

### Grouped sections

Use `2-3` sections depending on the day. Default section order:

- China policy and economy
- World
- Tech and companies only when the story set warrants it

For each section:

- Add a short section intro only when it adds information
- Include `2-3` stories
- Keep these items faster than the `Top 3`; do not turn every section item into a second lead
- Default item rhythm:
  - `What happened`
  - `Who it hits / why read`
  - `Source / date`
- Add a separate hard-fact line only when the number, decision, or actor is the core point of the item
- The grouped sections should feel like a quick scan, not a second batch of explainers
- Prefer variety inside the domestic section when the day allows it; a good issue often balances one rules/governance item, one real-economy or innovation item, and optionally one demand or public-life item

### 经济投资风向标

- Add a short fixed section after the grouped sections
- Default to `3-4` signals; use `2` on slow days and `4` when macro, rates, rules, or cost pressure clearly support it
- Prefer rates, currencies, policy tone, industry momentum, market risk appetite, commodity moves, data releases, or other macro signals with clear investor relevance
- For each signal include:
  - `Signal`
  - `What changed`
  - `Why it matters`
- Name each signal with a short label that a repeat reader can scan quickly
- Keep the tone directional and explanatory, not prescriptive
- When helpful, write the `Why it matters` line so it names what the reader should watch first

### What to watch today

- Add `3-5` concrete watch points for the same day
- Prefer launches, meetings, votes, deadlines, earnings, policy signals, or likely follow-up developments
- Each point should name the actor, trigger, or consequence to watch
- If helpful, include the likely time window or decision point

### Image suggestions

Include only when useful:

- `Cover image suggestion`
- `Section image suggestion`
- `Search prompt`

### Source summary

- List the main outlets or official sources used

## Guardrails

- Write as `《环球日报》`, not as a generic daily article.
- Prefer the most recent reliable update available by `08:00 Asia/Shanghai`.
- Avoid reusing yesterday's lead story unless there is a material overnight change.
- Keep the rhythm stable enough that readers recognize `《环球日报》` every day.
- Make the first screen useful even if the reader stops after the first scroll.
- Keep the top easy to enter, but let the body stay precise and editor-like.
- Let the lead and top 3 reflect the shared public agenda before narrower sector stories.
- Keep the `经济投资风向标` short and high-signal; never let it turn into stock picking.
- If a `Top 3` item still feels like a headline stub, add one hard fact and one next-step pressure before moving on.
- Do not let the whole article fall into one repeated sentence pattern.
- Prefer adding explanatory value over adding raw item count.

`toprss.ifeed.cc` is useful as a feed-discovery surface, not as a flat endorsement of source quality. Many entries are delivered through mirrors such as `plink.anyfeeder.com` or `rsshub.app`; treat those as transport layers and attribute the final article to the original publisher.

Use these to capture the shared domestic agenda first:

- 新华社 / 新华网
- 央视新闻 / 中国政府网
- 中国人民银行
- 国家统计局
- 财政部 / 海关总署 / 证监会 and other directly relevant agencies

These feeds are useful for discovery, gap detection, and agenda comparison. They are not evidence by themselves.

## Lead Selection Rules

- Do not let a niche company announcement lead the whole issue of `《环球日报》` unless it clearly changes markets, policy, platform power, or mainstream daily life.
- Prefer a China-relevant macro or public-interest lead when several stories compete.
- Use specialist business or tech outlets to improve framing, but not as the sole basis for a major headline if stronger primary or broad reporting exists.
- Use Chinese-language bridge feeds to improve transmission into a Chinese morning-news frame, but still confirm high-stakes stories with primary reporting, official releases, or multiple reputable outlets.
- If the day is light, it is acceptable for a high-impact company announcement to enter the top 3, but it should still be written in a broad reader-facing frame.

## Editorial Use

- Do not treat every company newsroom post as a lead story.
- Do not let sector media overwhelm the shared agenda; use them to improve framing and specificity after the lead is set.
- Do not confuse feed mirrors, ranking pages, or aggregator wrappers with the reporting source.
- Use official sources for confirmation, detail, and original framing.
- Keep `《环球日报》` balanced: public agenda first, sector detail second.
- If an official announcement matters only inside one product ecosystem, it should usually stay in a section rather than become the headline of the day.
- Use macro and policy sources to support the `经济投资风向标`, but keep the section explanatory rather than tactical.

## Maintenance

- Prefer feeds that are stable, machine-readable, and close to the original publisher.
- If a mirrored feed on `plink.anyfeeder.com` or `rsshub.app` is the most stable transport, keep it, but still record the publisher as the source of record.
- If a feed becomes noisy or unreliable, replace it with another source in the same editorial role instead of expanding the feed list without bound.
- If a source changes quality, adjust its tier before removing it entirely.

## source-policy.md
# Source Policy

## Source Priority

Use this order when possible:

1. Official announcements, filings, transcripts, and agency statements
2. Primary reporting from reputable outlets
3. Follow-up explainers for context
4. Aggregators only for discovery, not as the main cited source

## Feed Transport Rules

- Treat `toprss.ifeed.cc` as a discovery index, not as the editorial source of truth.
- If a feed arrives through `plink.anyfeeder.com`, `rsshub.app`, or another mirror, cite the original publisher in the article rather than the transport layer.
- Prefer original feeds when they are stable and complete; use mirrors as intake fallback, not attribution fallback.
- If a mirror strips timing, context, or source detail from a high-stakes story, go back to the publisher page or drop the item.

## Selection Rules

- Use mixed Chinese and English sources when that improves coverage.
- Prefer high-signal stories with clear public, policy, market, platform, or business impact.
- Merge duplicate angles into one item instead of repeating the same story.
- Skip low-value virality unless it reflects a larger shift worth explaining.
- Default to China-reader relevance when two stories look equally large on paper.
- Use ranking, digest, and hot-list feeds only to discover or prioritize candidates; they do not satisfy sourcing requirements by themselves.
- Use Chinese-language bridge feeds to improve transmission and framing, but do not let them replace stronger primary reporting on high-stakes international stories.
- For high-stakes cross-border `Top 3` items, prefer at least one primary or official source plus one corroborating report or follow-up source when available.

## Selection Rubric

Score each candidate story before drafting. Use a `0-2` score for each dimension.

### Positive dimensions

- `importance`: Does it affect policy, markets, major platforms, or public life?
- `china-reader relevance`: Will a Chinese WeChat reader feel this matters this morning?
- `freshness`: Did it materially happen or materially advance in the target window?
- `public-interest value`: Does it help explain the shared agenda of the day rather than just a niche corner?
- `explanatory value`: Does it help explain a larger shift, not just a one-off event?
- `section fit`: Does it strengthen one of the default sections?

### Negative dimensions

- `duplication penalty`: Is it just another angle on a story already selected?
- `noise penalty`: Is it mostly virality, outrage bait, or low-consequence chatter?

### Decision rule

- Strong keep: total score `8+` with no major sourcing problem
- Borderline: total score `6-7`; keep only if the news day is light or the item adds needed variety
- Drop: total score `5 or below`, or any item with unresolved credibility issues

Prefer `8-12` total stories for a normal full report, including the top 3. On slow days, go lower.

## Wind-Vane Rules

- Build `经济投资风向标` from macro, policy, rates, currency, commodity, sector-temperature, or risk-appetite signals.
- Prefer developments that change the direction of travel, not just routine price noise.
- Keep each signal short: what changed, what it suggests, and why a reader should care.
- Do not turn the section into explicit buy/sell advice or a list of stock picks.
- If the day has only one clean signal, keep the section to one strong point rather than padding it.

## Uncertainty Rules

- Mark fast-moving stories as `developing` when facts are still changing.
- Separate confirmed facts from claims, estimates, allegations, and predictions.
- If the available sourcing is weak or single-source, either qualify it clearly or drop the item.

## Attribution Rules

- Every story must include a source name and publish date.
- If multiple sources are used, cite the dominant reporting source and cross-check silently unless cross-checking changes the interpretation.
- Do not imply firsthand certainty when the article is based on reported information.
- Do not cite `AnyFeeder`, `RSSHub`, `TopRSS`, or another feed wrapper as if it were the newsroom or publisher.
- If a cross-check materially changes confidence or interpretation, surface that uncertainty in the copy instead of hiding it.

## Day Boundary Rules

- Default to the current `Asia/Shanghai` calendar day.
- If a major story started earlier but materially changed today, include it and note that it is a carry-over.

## Column Continuity Rules

- For the standing issue of `《环球日报》`, treat `08:00 Asia/Shanghai` as the publication cutoff.
- Prefer the latest reliable information available by the cutoff rather than waiting for perfect completeness.
- Avoid using the same lead story on consecutive days unless there is a clear overnight development.
- If a story continues across multiple days, change the angle to reflect what is newly important.
- Avoid repeating the same `经济投资风向标` conclusion on consecutive days unless the signal truly persisted and materially strengthened.

## golden-sample.md
# Golden Sample

Use this file as the style anchor for `《环球日报》`. Do not copy phrases mechanically; match the pacing, density, and editorial restraint.

For a concrete full article example, also read `references/global-daily-final-sample.md`.

## What Good Looks Like

- The title is direct and high-density.
- The title is followed by one light metadata line with the cutoff and, when helpful, the read time.
- The first screen tells the reader the day's three sharpest facts or stakes in one quick scan.
- The lead names the day's common thread instead of listing headlines.
- The top 3 stories each work as semi-explainers with facts, key detail, and transmission.
- The article moves from the biggest shared public signal to narrower sector stories without using the same event chain twice in `Top 3`.
- The `经济投资风向标` is short, specific, and directional.
- The close ends with a concrete watchlist, not a long conclusion.
- The first screen is broad-reader-friendly, but the body still feels like restrained editor copy rather than social posting.

## House Style

- Write in clean Simplified Chinese.
- Keep sentences moderately short.
- Prefer one idea per paragraph.
- Avoid filler transitions and dramatic adjectives.
- Sound like an informed editor, not a commentator trying to win an argument.
- Make the entry easy, but do not flatten the body into overly simple copy.

## Sample Shape

### Title

`《环球日报》｜3月9日`

### Metadata line

Put one light line under the title.

Example pattern:

`信息截至 2026 年 3 月 9 日 08:00（Asia/Shanghai）｜约 5 分钟读完`

### First-screen pattern

Put `先看三句` directly under the title.

Example pattern:

- `伊朗再次发动袭击，市场已经不再相信冲突会很快结束。`
- `油价和元件价格一起上行，外部冲击正在沿着成本链传导。`
- `信用、税务和数据规则继续收紧，国内经营环境还在被重新塑形。`

### Lead pattern

Start with one sentence that names the common thread of the day.
When possible, get one hard fact, named actor, or concrete stake onto the screen before the abstract synthesis.
The opening should feel like an easy entry for a broad reader, then quickly hand the reader into a denser newsroom-style body.

Example pattern:

`今天真正值得看的，不是某一条孤立刷屏的消息，而是政策、市场和国际风险三条线索正在同时抬高一天的判断门槛。`

Follow with one or two sentences that translate that pattern into reader value.

### Body pattern

For each major story:

- first block: what happened
- second block: concrete facts such as who did what, on what date, at what scale, or with what number
- third block: the specific point of tension, unresolved issue, or next step
- fourth block: why it matters now and where the effect transmits next

Keep these blocks compact but complete. The reader should understand the story without immediately needing another article. If a block does not contain at least one hard fact, it is probably still too abstract.
Do not let two `Top 3` items cover the same event chain unless the distinction is truly meaningful to the reader.
When a `Top 3` item is already strong, prefer adding one more layer such as `why now`, a transmission chain, or a market mechanism instead of adding weaker new items elsewhere.

For grouped-section items, compress the rhythm. Most of them should be readable as:

- what happened
- who it hits / why read
- source / date

### 风向标 pattern

Add `3-4` compact signals after the main sections when the day supports it.

For each signal:

- name the signal clearly
- say what changed
- explain why it matters to the direction of policy, markets, or risk appetite
- when useful, name what the reader should watch first
- stop before it turns into advice

### Watchlist pattern

End with `3-5` concrete watch points.

Example pattern:

`上午如果出现新的政策细则，市场会马上从方向判断切到节奏判断。`

# Global Daily Final Sample

Use this file as the concrete full-length style anchor for the standing `08:00` issue of `《环球日报》`.

## Sample Article

`《环球日报》｜3月9日`

*信息截至 2026 年 3 月 9 日 08:00（Asia/Shanghai）｜约 5 分钟读完*

### 先看三句

- 地缘风险没有退场，市场开始重新定价油价、运价和利率压力。
- 硬件链条的成本压力正在从芯片扩散到更基础的元件和物流环节。
- 国内经营环境继续向统一规则和数据化治理收束，规则变量正在变得更重要。

今天最值得连起来看的，不是几条孤立刷屏的 headline，而是地缘风险怎样通过油价、物流和元件价格一路传到企业成本端，同时国内的信用、税务和创新规则还在继续塑造经营环境。对《环球日报》的中国读者来说，重点不是记住所有 headline，而是判断哪些变量已经开始改变企业预算、融资预期和终端价格。

### 今天最重要的三件事

**1. 地缘风险升级后，市场不再只看战场，而是开始重算成本链**
发生了什么：如果当天的国际主线已经从军事对抗延伸到油价、航运和利率预期，它就不再只是“国际新闻”，而是在改写全球成本体系。
具体事实：一个合格的 `Top 3` 大条，至少要写清楚谁在行动、哪项能力仍然保留、油价或运价变动到什么水平，以及这种变化发生在什么时候。
卡点 / 下一步：接下来最值得盯的，不只是冲突会不会升级，而是相关航运预期、外交缓冲空间和风险偏好会不会一起恶化。
为什么重要 / 对谁有影响：先被影响的是能源、运输、出口链和市场风险偏好，随后才会回到更广义的制造成本和利率预期。
来源 / 日期：新华社，YYYY-MM-DD；Reuters，YYYY-MM-DD

**2. 国内规则建设继续推进，影响的不只是合规，更是融资与经营边界**
发生了什么：如果当天有信用、税务、评价体系或数据治理类文件出台，重要的往往不是表述本身，而是它会不会被接成可执行的规则链。
具体事实：一个好的写法，不只要交代文件说了什么，还要点出统一规则、公示渠道、信用修复或融资支持这些具体机制。
卡点 / 下一步：真正要看的是地方和部门能不能把数据共享、流程标准和法律配套尽快落地，否则就容易停留在口径统一。
