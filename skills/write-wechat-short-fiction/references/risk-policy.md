# Risk Policy

Read this before drafting from real-life material or sensitive subjects.

## Real People And Privacy

- Do not copy identifiable private individuals directly into the fiction.
- Composite or fictionalize distinguishing details when borrowing from lived experience.
- Remove exact employers, addresses, school names, hospitals, or chat screenshots unless the user clearly owns that material and wants them retained.

## Public Events

- Do not build a story on unverified breaking-news details.
- If a current event is only background atmosphere, keep it light and factual.
- If the facts are unstable, drop the explicit tie-in and keep the story fictional.

## Harm, Violence, And Trauma

- Avoid glamorized self-harm, graphic gore, or exploitative suffering.
- Treat coercion, assault, abuse, or death with restraint and only when the story truly needs it.
- Prefer implication over spectacle for WeChat-distributed fiction unless the user explicitly asks for darker material.

## Minors And Vulnerable People

- Use extra care with children, elderly people, patients, or financially precarious characters.
- Do not use vulnerable characters as emotional props without agency or texture.

## Social Categories

- Avoid diagnosis-as-metaphor, class caricature, and easy villainization of service workers or strangers.
- When writing across gender, class, age, or disability lines, favor observed behavior over explanatory labels.
