# Continuity Playbook

Use this file when the series has recent issues and the next story needs to feel fresh without breaking the overall identity.

## What to Scan

- Recent `5-10` issues if available.
- Whether a serial arc is already in progress and which episode comes next.
- Repeated protagonist archetypes.
- Repeated settings, anchor objects, and ending modes.
- Repeated sentence rhythm or repeated emotional temperature.

## Freshness Axes

Change at least two of these when recent stories feel adjacent:

- protagonist relation to power: subordinate, service role, customer, parent, neighbor, ex-partner, freelancer, student, retiree
- story engine: errand, lie, debt, return, waiting, wrong delivery, omission, coincidence, small betrayal
- setting density: confined interior, threshold, moving transit, public queue, family room, rainy exterior
- narrative distance: first person, third person tight, observer narrator
- time pressure: same-night, next-morning, deadline, train departure, closing time, holiday arrival
- ending mode: reveal, reversal, silence, object remains, action suspended

## Guardrails

- Keep the same overall reading contract: contemporary, clear, human-scale, phone-readable.
- If a serial arc is in progress, preserve plot facts and episode order before chasing freshness.
- Do not chase novelty so hard that the series starts genre-hopping wildly.
- When changing many surface details, keep one familiar series strength such as a precise opening or a quiet ending.
