# Default Template

Use this structure for the standard issue. Treat it as the default scaffold, not a rigid cage.

## Frontmatter

```markdown
---
title: 《短篇小说｜YYYY.MM.DD｜篇名》
cover: ./assets/default-cover.jpg
---
```

## Narrative Rhythm

### Part 1: opening disturbance

- `100-180` Chinese characters.
- Open on a concrete place, object, and action.
- Put one unstable element on the page early: a missing item, delayed message, social pressure, small lie, deadline, mistaken identity, or private dread.

### Part 2: pressure builds

- `220-340` Chinese characters.
- Let the protagonist try something, avoid something, or misread something.
- Keep exposition short and attached to present movement.

### Part 3: turn or decision

- `320-520` Chinese characters.
- Force a choice, reveal hidden information, or let a small action become costly.
- Keep the cast focused. New characters should sharpen the same plot pressure.

### Part 4: landing

- `140-260` Chinese characters.
- End on the cost, residue, reversal, or lingering image created by the turn.
- Do not explain the lesson.

## Assembly Rules

- Aim for `5-9` paragraphs by default. Allow `4-12` only when rhythm improves.
- Keep paragraphs mobile-friendly. Prefer `1-3` sentences each.
- Keep one stable point of view per issue unless the user explicitly requests a structural experiment.
- Keep direct dialogue below roughly one quarter of the story unless dialogue is the engine.
- If the story starts collapsing into summary or contains more than one major turn, decide between an `extended issue` and `references/serialization-playbook.md` instead of squeezing plot into exposition.
- If the user gives a fixed title, series name, cover path, or character sheet, replace the defaults and keep the rest of the structure.
- Do not add section headings to the final article.
- Do not append explanation, writing notes, or publishing instructions.
