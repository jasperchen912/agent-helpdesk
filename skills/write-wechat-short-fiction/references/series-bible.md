# Series Bible

Use this file to keep the fiction series stable across issues.

## Core Definition

This series is a recurring WeChat short-fiction column built for mobile reading. Each issue or installment should feel complete in one sitting: one clear story movement, one emotional pressure, one aftertaste.

## Editorial Promise

- Give readers a full short-story experience without asking for novel-length patience.
- Stay specific enough to feel lived, but clear enough to read on a phone.
- Keep the plot human-scale even when the story carries mystery, coincidence, or light uncanniness.
- Leave residue rather than a lecture.

## What the Series Is

- Contemporary fiction rooted in ordinary spaces, social pressure, delayed messages, small lies, narrow escapes, awkward obligations, and quiet turns.
- Character-close writing that lets behavior and objects carry theme.
- Stories that can be shared in a WeChat feed without feeling like chopped-up novel chapters, even when a longer premise is split into installments.

## What the Series Is Not

- Not an opinion essay disguised as fiction.
- Not a pure twist machine with no emotional setup.
- Not a motivational parable.
- Not a diary that depends on private backstory the reader cannot access.
- Not high-concept worldbuilding unless the user explicitly asks for stronger genre elements.

## Narrative Rules

- Put a place, an object, and an unstable situation on the page early.
- Keep one dominant engine per story: missed connection, secret, errand, debt, waiting, return, lie, misrecognition, or small system failure.
- Give the protagonist one readable desire or fear, even if they never say it aloud.
- Let at least one detail feel impossible to transplant into another story unchanged.
- If the story grows beyond the comfortable one-post rhythm, choose between an extended issue and a serial before drafting.
- Use exposition only when scene and action cannot carry the needed information alone.

## Ending Rules

- Earn the ending through setup rather than concealment.
- Favor a reveal, choice, cost, residue, or reframed image over a thesis.
- In serial mode, let each installment close one local movement before exposing the next pressure.
- Keep the last paragraph local. Do not zoom out into a statement about life, society, or humanity.

## Variety Levers

Rotate at least two of these when adjacent issues feel too similar:

- `story engine`: errand, delay, debt, lie, return, waiting, wrong delivery, small disappearance
- `social distance`: alone, family, neighbor, ex-colleague, stranger, service worker, platform contact
- `setting`: home interior, threshold, transit, office, street, shop, hospital corridor, hotel, school gate
- `narrator distance`: first person close, third person tight, restrained omniscient only if necessary
- `ending mode`: reveal, reversal, suspended action, object remains, silence after speech

## Revision Priorities

1. Clarify what the protagonist wants or fears.
2. Sharpen the concrete pressure in the scene.
3. Remove explanation that repeats what action already shows.
4. Strengthen the ending by cutting slogans before cutting plot.
