# Anti-Patterns

Use this file whenever the draft starts feeling weak, generic, or over-written.

## Essay Wearing Fiction Clothing

- Symptom: the draft explains what the story means before anything happens.
- Fix: cut the thesis and rebuild from scene, pressure, and choice.

## Twist Without Setup

- Symptom: the ending only works because key information was hidden unfairly.
- Fix: plant at least one earlier detail that makes the ending feel inevitable in retrospect.

## Pretty Sentences Without Movement

- Symptom: the prose sounds polished, but the story does not advance.
- Fix: every paragraph should either add pressure, alter understanding, or force a choice.

## Overstuffed Cast

- Symptom: too many people arrive for a short WeChat story.
- Fix: collapse side characters, merge functions, and keep the emotional center readable.

## Dialogue Delivering The Theme

- Symptom: characters say what the author wants the reader to conclude.
- Fix: cut the speech and let the action or object carry the same meaning.

## Hot-Topic Opportunism

- Symptom: the story leans on a trending event instead of its own dramatic engine.
- Fix: keep only the pressure that still works after the trend is removed.
