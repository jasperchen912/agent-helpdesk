# Series Bible

Use this file to keep the column identity stable across weeks and months.

## Core Definition

`《浴室沉思》` is a nightly urban reflective essay, not a diary and not a commentary column. Start from one bodily or spatial moment late in the day. End in a shared recognition that still feels local and lived.

## Editorial Promise

- Give readers one small place to settle at night.
- Turn private residue into public recognizability.
- Offer companionship without consolation language.
- Leave an aftertaste, not a conclusion.

## What the Series Is

- A column about late-day residue: pause, awkwardness, slight fatigue, delayed recognition, tiny social distance.
- Written from close range: one room, one queue, one elevator, one bus stop, one convenience store aisle, one screen glow spilling into a physical space.
- Built on observation first, reflection second.

## What the Series Is Not

- Not a motivational column.
- Not a therapy transcript.
- Not a public-affairs opinion piece.
- Not a pure personal diary that depends on private backstory.
- Not a collection of quotable sentences pretending to be an essay.

## Voice Rules

- Keep the narrator warm but unsentimental.
- Allow `我`, but make the piece readable to someone who was not there.
- Prefer clean, direct sentences with occasional longer sentences for breath.
- Let uncertainty remain uncertainty. Do not over-explain motives or readers.
- Keep emotional temperature low-to-medium even when the topic carries pressure.

## Non-Negotiables

- Put one concrete image in the first two sentences.
- Keep one dominant scene family so the essay has a physical center.
- Make at least one sentence specific enough that it could not be pasted into any other reflective essay unchanged.
- Keep the last paragraph local. Do not widen it into life advice or a thesis about humanity.

## Variety Levers

Rotate at least two of these when the recent issues feel too similar:

- `scene family`: home interior, building threshold, transit, micro-public interior, digital residue entering physical space
- `anchor object`: key, umbrella, receipt, sink, towel, freezer door, elevator button, bus pole, half-written message
- `human distance`: alone, stranger, acquaintance, neighbor, family member, anonymous crowd
- `motion type`: waiting, washing, carrying, deleting, stepping off, looking away, locking, pausing
- `ending mode`: object remains, sound stops, light changes, body relaxes, action is suspended

## Revision Priorities

1. Clarify the scene.
2. Lower the emotional volume if it feels inflated.
3. Sharpen the title focus.
4. Soften the ending by cutting explanation before cutting imagery.
