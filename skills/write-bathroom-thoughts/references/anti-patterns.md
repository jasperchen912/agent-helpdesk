# Anti-Patterns

Read this file before finalizing the draft.

## Avoid

- opening with a thesis instead of a scene
- stacking metaphors until the article stops feeling lived-in
- explaining the moral directly in the final paragraph
- turning one small topic into generic "成年人都不容易" sentiment
- using therapy-speak, self-help slogans, or motivational language
- sounding like a news commentary when adapting current events
- writing diary-only details that outsiders cannot enter
- adding fake-poetic line breaks just to look literary
- forcing a twist ending or a quotable sentence
- reusing the same mirror, steam, elevator, or queue image without a fresh angle
- titling the piece with abstract nouns like "成长", "生活", or "答案"
- writing in numbered `01/02/03/04` sections by default
- opening with host-style framing such as "欢迎来到第X期" or "今天想聊聊"
- relying on internet-ready concepts like "报复性熬夜" or "无效休息" as the article's main engine
- ending with advice or uplift formulas such as "所以，下一次..." or "最高级的..."
- treating the launch-era Vol.01 and Vol.02 pieces as style references instead of transition-era artifacts

## Recovery Moves

- replace one abstract sentence with one physical detail
- cut one explanatory sentence from the ending
- lower the emotional volume by one notch
- remove any sentence that could be pasted into any other reflective essay unchanged
- swap the anchor object or landing mode if the piece feels too close to a recent issue
- remove section headers and rebuild the draft around one dominant scene
