# Topic Selection

Use this file when the user does not provide a topic or when deciding whether a current event can be adapted into the series.

## Priority Order

1. Use the user's trigger word, image, or incident if one is provided.
2. If nothing is provided, choose one small evening scene with built-in emotional residue.
3. Only use a public event when the user requests it or when the event clearly mirrors an everyday emotional pattern.

## Good Self-Selected Topics

- waiting in a line that suddenly becomes quiet
- washing hands after getting home and noticing the day's last tension
- the mirror light in a rental bathroom
- hearing neighbors close doors one after another
- deleting a half-written message before sleep
- convenience store aisles after 9 PM
- the hesitation before stepping off the last bus
- a wet umbrella leaning against the wall before it is put away
- standing in front of the freezer door while deciding whether to buy tomorrow's breakfast
- the office pantry after most lights have already gone out
- the click of keys on the table after getting home too late

## Weak Topics

- broad life lessons with no scene
- obviously inspirational subjects chosen for easy morals
- private backstory that needs too much explanation
- purely online hot takes with no bodily or spatial anchor
- another mirror-or-steam piece with no fresh social pressure or formal change

## Adapting Current Events

- Verify unstable facts first.
- Use the event as a small pressure change in the background, not as the article's main analytical object.
- Narrow quickly from the public event to one ordinary moment it illuminates.
- Cut the event angle if the piece starts sounding like a column, explainer, or social critique.

## Selection Test

Choose the topic only if the answer is yes to most of these:

- Can it be opened with one clear image?
- Can it hold `700-1200` Chinese characters without padding?
- Can the reflection stay specific rather than universal?
- Can the ending remain quiet?
- Can it differ from recent issues in scene family, anchor object, or landing mode?
