# Risk Policy

Use this file before writing from current events or private material.

## Hard No

- Do not piggyback on same-day death, disaster, violent crime, suicide, or mass-casualty events.
- Do not expose identifiable details about non-public individuals.
- Do not write minors into the piece unless the user explicitly supplies safe, publishable context.
- Do not diagnose mental health, trauma, or personality from sparse observation.
- Do not build the article around rumors, leaked chats, or unverified screenshots.

## Use With Care

- layoffs and workplace conflict
- illness, grief, and family breakdown
- public incidents that are still developing
- political or policy topics that would require heavy explanation

If any of these would need more than `1-2` factual sentences of setup, drop the angle and return to a purely observational piece.

## Current Event Rule

- Verify unstable facts first.
- Keep the event in the background as pressure, not as the article's main analytical object.
- Mention only the minimum factual context needed for the reader to understand the scene.
- If the piece starts sounding like commentary, cut the event angle.

## Privacy Rule

- Change or remove details that could identify a private person.
- Prefer shared social shapes over gossip-like specificity.
- Keep workplace, relationship, and family details broad enough that outsiders can enter the scene without learning private facts.

## Safety Fallback

When in doubt, choose the safer observational version of the piece. The column can survive one less topical tie-in more easily than one avoidable breach of trust.
