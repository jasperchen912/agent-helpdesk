# Global Daily Template

Use this template for the standing daily WeChat column `《环球日报》` published at `08:00` China time.

## Best Use Cases

- `《环球日报》` daily issue
- Stable recurring morning edition
- Habit-forming daily newsroom rhythm

## Structure

1. Fixed title in the format `《环球日报》｜X月X日`
2. One light metadata line with information cutoff, and optional read-time estimate
3. One fixed first-screen module: `先看三句`
4. One short lead that explains the day's main signal
5. `Today's 3 most important stories`
6. `2-3` grouped sections for the rest of the article
7. One fixed `经济投资风向标` section
8. `What to watch today`
9. Optional image suggestions
10. Source summary

## Article Skeleton

### Title

- Use the exact title pattern `《环球日报》｜X月X日` unless the user overrides it

### Metadata line

- Put one light metadata line under the title
- Default content:
  - information cutoff
  - optional read-time estimate when helpful
- Keep the tone neutral and useful, not promotional

### 先看三句

- Put this directly under the title
- Use exactly `3` short bullets or lines
- Each line should contain one concrete fact, stake, or shift, not a vague mood summary
- Make the block readable in about `10` seconds on a phone
- Do not simply repeat the lead in shorter wording
- When the day supports it, prefer this three-part mix:
  - one shared-agenda or conflict fact
  - one market, cost, or transmission fact
  - one domestic rule, policy, or public-value fact

### Lead

- One short paragraph
- Explain the common thread of the day
- Front-load one hard fact, named actor, or concrete stake before abstract synthesis when possible
- Frame why a China-based morning reader should care now
- The lead should be easy to enter for a broad reader but still point toward the more editor-like logic of the body

### Today's 3 most important stories

For each item include:

- `Headline`
- `What happened`: one short factual block that states the new development cleanly
- `Concrete facts`: one short block with the key actor, figure, timing, or decision
- `Key friction / next step`: one short block that says what is unresolved, what changed overnight, or what the reader should watch next
- `Why it matters / who it hits`: one short block that explains where the impact transmits next and who feels it first
- `Source / date`

Treat `Top 3` as concrete information blocks, not as compressed clips. They should be visibly more detailed than the grouped-section items and should contain named actors plus at least one hard fact.
One event chain should usually occupy only one `Top 3` slot. If two candidate items are really the same story with different angles, merge them into one stronger explainer.
Let item `1` feel like the clear lead and let items `2-3` widen the reader's picture of the day rather than echo it.
If the day supports it, enrich major `Top 3` items with one more layer such as `why now`, a transmission chain, or a market mechanism before adding extra low-value items elsewhere.

### Grouped sections

Use `2-3` sections depending on the day. Default section order:

- China policy and economy
- World
- Tech and companies only when the story set warrants it

For each section:

- Add a short section intro only when it adds information
- Include `2-3` stories
- Keep these items faster than the `Top 3`; do not turn every section item into a second lead
- Default item rhythm:
  - `What happened`
  - `Who it hits / why read`
  - `Source / date`
- Add a separate hard-fact line only when the number, decision, or actor is the core point of the item
- The grouped sections should feel like a quick scan, not a second batch of explainers
- Prefer variety inside the domestic section when the day allows it; a good issue often balances one rules/governance item, one real-economy or innovation item, and optionally one demand or public-life item

### 经济投资风向标

- Add a short fixed section after the grouped sections
- Default to `3-4` signals; use `2` on slow days and `4` when macro, rates, rules, or cost pressure clearly support it
- Prefer rates, currencies, policy tone, industry momentum, market risk appetite, commodity moves, data releases, or other macro signals with clear investor relevance
- For each signal include:
  - `Signal`
  - `What changed`
  - `Why it matters`
- Name each signal with a short label that a repeat reader can scan quickly
- Keep the tone directional and explanatory, not prescriptive
- When helpful, write the `Why it matters` line so it names what the reader should watch first

### What to watch today

- Add `3-5` concrete watch points for the same day
- Prefer launches, meetings, votes, deadlines, earnings, policy signals, or likely follow-up developments
- Each point should name the actor, trigger, or consequence to watch
- If helpful, include the likely time window or decision point

### Image suggestions

Include only when useful:

- `Cover image suggestion`
- `Section image suggestion`
- `Search prompt`

### Source summary

- List the main outlets or official sources used

## Guardrails

- Write as `《环球日报》`, not as a generic daily article.
- Prefer the most recent reliable update available by `08:00 Asia/Shanghai`.
- Avoid reusing yesterday's lead story unless there is a material overnight change.
- Keep the rhythm stable enough that readers recognize `《环球日报》` every day.
- Make the first screen useful even if the reader stops after the first scroll.
- Keep the top easy to enter, but let the body stay precise and editor-like.
- Let the lead and top 3 reflect the shared public agenda before narrower sector stories.
- Keep the `经济投资风向标` short and high-signal; never let it turn into stock picking.
- If a `Top 3` item still feels like a headline stub, add one hard fact and one next-step pressure before moving on.
- Do not let the whole article fall into one repeated sentence pattern.
- Prefer adding explanatory value over adding raw item count.
