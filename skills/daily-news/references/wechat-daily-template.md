# WeChat Daily Template

Use this template for the default full daily report. Render the final article in Simplified Chinese.

## Structure

1. Three title candidates
2. One short lead paragraph
3. `Today's 3 most important stories`
4. `2-4` grouped sections for the rest of the report
5. One short `经济投资风向标` section
6. Image suggestions for the cover and each section
7. Source summary at the end

## Article Skeleton

### Title candidates

- Candidate 1
- Candidate 2
- Candidate 3

### Lead

- One short paragraph that explains the day's overall mood or signal.
- Frame the day for a Chinese WeChat reader rather than for a global-news insider.

### Today's 3 most important stories

For each item include:

- `Headline`
- `What happened`: one concise factual paragraph
- `Concrete facts`: one short paragraph on the actor, number, timing, or explicit decision
- `Key friction / next step`: one short paragraph on the unresolved point, constraint, or what to watch next
- `Why it matters`: one short paragraph on the policy, market, platform, or social transmission
- `Source / date`

These `Top 3` items should be the most complete parts of the article. Let them run longer than ordinary section items and make them concrete enough that the reader can picture the moving parts.

### Grouped sections

Use `2-4` sections depending on the day. Typical sections:

- Domestic policy and economy
- World and geopolitics
- Technology and companies when broadly relevant
- Culture or platform shifts only when the impact is clear

For each section:

- Add a short section intro
- Include `2-4` stories
- Keep each story in the same `Headline / What happened / Why it matters / Source / date` structure
- Keep these section items compact so the whole article does not bloat evenly.

### 经济投资风向标

- Add `2-4` compact signals after the grouped sections
- Focus on macro, policy, markets, sector temperature, or risk appetite
- Explain the direction of travel and why the signal matters
- Do not turn the section into trading advice or stock recommendations

### Image suggestions

Include:

- `Cover image suggestion`: one sentence
- `Section image suggestion`: one sentence per section
- `Search prompt`: a short prompt for image search or illustration generation

### Source summary

- List the source names used in the article
- Keep the list concise and readable

## Guardrails

- Prefer fewer stories over filler.
- Do not let commentary exceed the factual summary.
- Keep the piece readable as a single WeChat article, not a bulletin board.
- Let the shared public agenda come before narrow company or product coverage.
