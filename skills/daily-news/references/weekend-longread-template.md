# Weekend Longread Template

Use this template for a slower, more synthesized weekend piece. This is for users who want a readable article built around a few strong threads rather than a day-by-day list.

## Best Use Cases

- Weekend newsletter
- Weekly wrap with a daily-news tone
- "What actually mattered this week"
- Readers who want context more than velocity

## Structure

1. One title or up to three title candidates
2. One opening lead that states the week's main theme
3. `2-3` major threads
4. `3-6` supporting stories across those threads
5. One short closing judgment
6. Source summary

## Article Skeleton

### Title

- Keep it direct but slightly broader than a daily title
- Example pattern: `《这一周，最该看清的几件事》`

### Lead

- One short paragraph that defines the week's underlying pattern

### Major threads

For each thread include:

- `Thread label`
- `What changed this week`
- `Why it matters`
- `2-3` supporting stories with source/date

### Closing judgment

- End with one compact editorial sentence on what this week revealed

### Source summary

- List the main sources used

## Guardrails

- Do not turn this into a chronological diary.
- Synthesize multiple items into larger narratives.
- Allow slightly more context than the daily formats, but keep the tone restrained.
- Prefer `2-3` strong threads over many small items.
