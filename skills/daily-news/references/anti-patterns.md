# Anti-Patterns

Use this file as a final quality filter. If the draft shows any of these patterns, revise before finishing.

## Bad Output Patterns

- `headline pile`: the piece reads like unrelated clips with no shared lead or ordering logic
- `template drift`: the article forgets the fixed `《环球日报》` rhythm and turns into a generic digest
- `empty first screen`: the opening uses abstract judgment but no hard fact, named actor, or concrete stake
- `same-story double count`: two `Top 3` slots are really one event chain told twice
- `source flattening`: a niche company blog post gets treated like a world-level headline without broader justification
- `tech bias`: AI or company updates dominate the piece on a day when the shared agenda clearly lies elsewhere
- `thin top 3`: the lead stories give conclusions but not enough detail, background, or transmission for a morning reader
- `floaty top 3`: the lead stories talk about趋势、预期、压力 but do not name actors, dates, numbers, or concrete next steps
- `filler padding`: weak items are added just to hit a target count
- `commentary bloat`: the opinion is longer than the factual summary
- `China-reader mismatch`: the framing is readable in English media terms but awkward for a Chinese WeChat audience
- `repeat framing`: the same point is repeated in the lead, body, and close without adding information
- `block sameness`: every item uses the same sentence length and block rhythm, making the piece feel machine-flat
- `section blur`: business, policy, and tech items are mixed together without a reason
- `watchlist fog`: the closing watchlist names broad topics but not the actor, trigger, or consequence
- `wind-vane cosplay`: the `经济投资风向标` turns into market gossip, stock tips, or generic “关注机会”
- `carry-over laziness`: yesterday's story is reused as today's lead without a materially new angle
- `false certainty`: a developing or weakly sourced story is written as settled fact

## Quick Fix Rules

- If the lead cannot name the day's common thread in one paragraph, narrow the story set.
- If the first screen cannot be scanned in `10` seconds, tighten it to three cleaner lines with one hard fact each.
- If two `Top 3` items belong to the same chain, merge them and use the freed slot for another public-value line.
- If the top 3 feel interchangeable, reorder by China-reader relevance, public impact, and reader value.
- If the top 3 feel too thin, strengthen them before adding more section items.
- If a `Top 3` item cannot answer “谁、做了什么、规模多大、下一步看什么”, it is still not concrete enough.
- If an item is interesting only to insiders, move it into a section or cut it.
- If a source is weak, qualify it or remove the story.
- If the article feels long, cut low-signal items before shortening the strong ones.
- If the article feels flat, strengthen the lead and `why it matters` lines instead of adding more stories.
- If the article feels monotonous, tighten section items into `发生了什么 + 影响谁 / 为什么看` instead of repeating the full `Top 3` rhythm.
- If the watchlist points read like vague reminders, rewrite them around actor + trigger + consequence.
- If the `经济投资风向标` feels generic, replace it with fewer but sharper signals.

## Final Check

Before shipping, confirm:

- one clear lead
- one useful first screen
- stable `《环球日报》` structure
- macro and public agenda first
- top 3 that are clearly more detailed than the rest of the article
- top 3 with no double counting of the same story chain
- top 3 with named actors, hard facts, and one clear next-step pressure
- one distinct `经济投资风向标` section
- no filler
- every major item has `source / date`
