# Economic And Investment Wind-Vane

Use this file when building the fixed `经济投资风向标` section for `《环球日报》`.

## Purpose

This section is not for stock tips. It exists to tell readers which macro, policy, and market signals are changing the direction of travel this morning.

## Placement

- Put the section after the main grouped sections.
- Put it before `What to watch today`.
- Keep it distinct from the top 3. Do not hide the wind-vane logic inside unrelated stories.

## Daily Contract

- Default to `3` signals.
- On a slow day, keep only `1-2` if that is all the day can support.
- Each signal should contain:
  - `Signal`
  - `What changed`
  - `Why it matters`
- Name each signal with a short scan-friendly label, such as an asset, variable, or pressure point.

## Good Signal Types

- policy tone or policy follow-through
- rates, liquidity, and credit conditions
- currency direction or cross-border capital sensitivity
- commodity moves with inflation or cost implications
- sector temperature and earnings sensitivity
- market risk appetite and defensive rotation
- key data or meeting setup that may move expectations later in the day

## Writing Rules

- Explain direction, not tactics.
- Prefer “what this suggests” over “what to buy”.
- Keep each item short and specific.
- Reframe the body into direction-of-travel language; do not just re-summarize the same body point with slightly different wording.
- Use terms like `risk appetite`, `policy tone`, `pricing pressure`, `sector temperature`, or `expectation reset` only when the underlying reporting supports them.
- If the signal is thin, say less instead of reaching.

## Failure Modes

- Do not write this section like a brokerage note.
- Do not pad it with routine price noise or generic optimism.
- Do not repeat the same conclusion every day without a fresh driver.
- Do not let it become a second summary of the article body with no new framing value.
