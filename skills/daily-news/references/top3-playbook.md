# Top 3 Playbook

Use this file when drafting the `Top 3` for `《环球日报》`.

## Purpose

The `Top 3` should do more than summarize headlines. Each item should leave the reader knowing what changed, which concrete facts matter, where the friction sits, who gets hit first, and where the impact is likely to show up next.

Before drafting the `Top 3`, check whether two candidates are really the same event chain. If they are, merge them into one stronger item and free the slot for a different line of the day.

## Default Structure

For each `Top 3` item, write four compact blocks:

1. `What happened`
2. `Concrete facts`
3. `Key friction / next step`
4. `Why it matters / who it hits`

## Minimum Specificity

Every `Top 3` item should contain at least `2` concrete anchors from this list:

- one named actor, institution, or country
- one number, price, size, rate, target, or date
- one explicit action, decision, or statement
- one next-step deadline, meeting, vote, route, or unresolved pressure

If you cannot name those anchors, the item is still too abstract.

## What To Put In The Middle Blocks

Use the second and third blocks to add one or two of the following:

- the detail that changed the story overnight
- the concrete number or decision that makes the item real
- the unresolved point or friction
- the next-step trigger or deadline
- the background needed to understand why the item matters now
- the specific policy, market, business, or public-life channel through which the story travels

Do not dump generic backstory. Include only the context needed to make the item legible this morning.

## Writing Rules

- Let the first block stay factual and current.
- Let the second block carry the hard facts: numbers, named actors, or the specific decision.
- Let the third block name the unresolved pressure, next step, or thing to watch.
- Let the fourth block answer: why should a Chinese WeChat reader care this morning, and who feels the effect first?
- Keep each block short enough to scan on a phone, but never vague.
- Prefer concrete transmission words such as `成本`, `预期`, `流动性`, `出口`, `风险偏好`, `监管压力`, `平台权力`, or `供应链` when they are truly supported.
- Item `1` should usually feel like the day's clearest shared agenda item.

## Boundary With Other Sections

- The `Top 3` should be the most detailed part of the article.
- Grouped-section items can stay compact.
- If the article feels too long, cut weaker section items before thinning the `Top 3`.

## Failure Modes

- Do not split one story chain across two `Top 3` slots just to create the appearance of variety.
- Do not turn every item into a mini editorial.
- Do not let the second and third block repeat the same idea with different wording.
- Do not add vague context such as `市场持续关注` or `各方普遍认为` unless you name the concrete pressure behind it.
- Do not write a `Top 3` item without at least one actor and one concrete number or date unless the story genuinely has none.
